# Creighton University Biophysical Optics Research Group (CU-BORG)

Welcome to the official repository for CU-BORG at Creighton University's Department of Physics. Our research focuses on the development of optical techinques applied to biological and medical physics.

## 🔬 Some examples of past and current research projects include
*   **Metabolic Imaging:** Using dual-channel multiphoton excited endogenous fluorescence intensity and fluorescence lifetime imaging (FLIM) to observe antibiotic-induced hearing loss and develop an all-optical biopsy for cancer.
*   **Cellular Biomechanics:** Quantifying mechanical properties and viscoelasticity of cells using dual-beam optical stretchers (in collaboration with Dr. Andrew Ekpenyong).
*   **Fluorescence Correlation Spectroscopy (FCS):** Investigating molecular configurations of GFP and tryptophan-containing proteins to decode cryopreservation mechanisms.
*   **Single Molecule Biophysics:** Particularly focused on determining membrane protein stoicheometry
